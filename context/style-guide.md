# TheHues Style Guide

This guide defines writing conventions, formatting standards, and editorial rules for TheHues content.

## Grammar & Mechanics

### Capitalization

**Headlines & Subheadings**:
- **Decision**: Sentence case for H2/H3 in blog body; title case acceptable for SEO titles and page headlines.

**Brand & Product Names**:
- TheHues (always capitalized as one word)
- custom curtains (generic term, lower case unless title position)
- blackout curtains / thermal curtains / soundproof curtains (lower case in body copy)

### Numbers

- Spell out one through nine in narrative text; use numerals for 10+.
- Always use numerals for dimensions, percentages, money, and product specs.
- Use US formatting: `1,000`, `$90`, `2-4 business days`.

### Punctuation

- **Oxford comma**: Yes.
- **Dashes**: Use em dashes sparingly for parenthetical emphasis.
- **Quotation marks**: Use standard double quotes.
- **Ellipses**: Use `...` sparingly.

### Abbreviations & Acronyms

- Spell out uncommon terms on first use.
- Keep common terms concise when audience familiarity is high.
- Avoid excessive acronym density in consumer-facing content.

---

## Word Choice & Usage

### Preferred Terms

**Say This** -> **Not That**
- custom curtains -> normal curtains (when discussing custom value)
- blackout curtains -> dark curtains
- thermal curtains -> warm curtains
- soundproof curtains -> quiet curtains
- header style -> curtain top thing
- measurement guide -> sizing tips only
- panel -> piece (unless simplifying for beginners)

### Avoid
- Overpromising claims not supported on site/policy pages
- Legal-entity references in blog writing (per user requirement)
- Generic CTA phrases like "click here"
- Dense jargon without explanation

### Inclusive/Readable Language
- Write in plain US English.
- Use second person (you/your) for direct guidance.
- Keep accessibility in mind with clear labels and meaningful anchors.

---

## Formatting Standards

### Text Emphasis
- **Bold**: Key takeaways and decision criteria.
- **Italics**: Rare, only for subtle emphasis.
- **ALL CAPS**: Avoid except in established UI labels.

### Lists
- Use numbered lists for steps.
- Use bullets for options/comparisons.
- Keep list items parallel and concise.

### Links
- Use descriptive anchor text.
- Prefer links to specific relevant pages (collection, guide, policy), not generic pages.
- External links should add clear user value.

---

## Content Structure

### Introductions
Standard intro pattern:
1. user problem/context
2. what decision this content helps with
3. what the reader will do next

### Section Length
- Aim for 120-300 words per sub-section in guides.
- Break long sections with scannable subheads and lists.

### Conclusions
- Recap practical decision points.
- Include one clear next-step CTA.

---

## SEO-Specific Style

### Meta Titles
- 50-60 characters preferred
- Include primary keyword early
- Avoid filler words and empty branding repetition

### Meta Descriptions
- 140-160 characters preferred
- Include value proposition + action cue
- Must read naturally

### URL Slugs
- lower-case
- hyphen-separated
- concise and intent-specific

### Alt Text
- Describe what the image shows
- Include keyword context only when natural
- Avoid keyword stuffing

---

## Dates & Time

- **Date format**: Month DD, YYYY (US standard)
- **Time format**: 12-hour with time zone when relevant (e.g., 5:00 p.m. EST)

---

## Brand-Specific Rules

1. Keep the voice premium but practical.
2. Keep guidance actionable, not aspirational-only.
3. Keep policy-related language consistent with the latest policy pages.
4. Do not include company legal-entity information in blog-facing copy.

---

## Editing Checklist

- [ ] Tone matches TheHues (calm, practical, design-aware)
- [ ] Terminology is consistent
- [ ] Instructions are clear and sequence-based
- [ ] Internal links are descriptive and relevant
- [ ] No unsupported claims
- [ ] No legal-entity details in public blog copy
- [ ] English + US audience assumptions are consistent
